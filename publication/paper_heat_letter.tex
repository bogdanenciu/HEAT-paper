\begin{abstract}
We test a \emph{single closed-form, parameter-free observable}
for the late-type size--redshift relation at fixed baryonic
mass,
\[
  \frac{R_{\heat}(z)}{R_{0}}
   \;=\;(1+z)^{-3/4}\,\times\,
   \left[\frac{\Omega_{m}(z)}{\Omega_{m}(0)}\right]^{1/4},
\]
in which exponent and normalisation enter as separately testable
factors.  The expression follows from the deep-MOND equilibrium
$R\propto\azero^{-1/2}$ combined with the empirically forced
scaling $\azero(z)\propto H(z)$: within the modified-inertia
family $\azero\propto H^{n}$, the late-type size exponent
measured by \citet{vanderWel2014} pins $n=1.00\pm 0.07$, leaving
no further freedom in the redshift evolution.  Horizon
thermodynamics interprets $n=1$ via the FRW apparent-horizon
temperature \citep{BakRey2000,CaiKim2005} and
\citet{Milgrom1999}'s identification
$\azero(0)=cH_{0}/(2\pi)\!\approx\!1.04\times 10^{-10}\,
\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$, matching the empirical MOND scale to within
$13\%$ \citep{Lelli2016,Li2018}.  We call this hypothesis
Hubble-Emergent Acceleration Theory (HEAT).
(i)~The first factor is the matter-era asymptote, matching the
empirical late-type size--redshift exponent measured in
thousands of galaxies by \citet{vanderWel2014} and confirmed at
higher redshift by \citet{Allen2017}.  (ii)~The second factor
is the closed-form normalisation correction, bounded and
monotonic, rising from unity at $z=0$ to
$\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.335$ in the matter era and fixed
entirely by the Planck expansion history.  Current data are
consistent with $\mathcal{R}(z)$ at the $\sim 20$--$30\%$
precision set by local size-calibration systematics but do not
yet resolve its distinctive rise above the matter-era power
law; homogeneous Euclid and Roman samples will reduce the
per-bin uncertainty to the percent level, at which point
$\mathcal{R}(z)$ becomes a quantitative test of the
$\azero\propto H(z)$ coupling within the $\lcdm$ expansion
history.  As an internal consistency check (not a statistical
test, given $N\!=\!4$), the same zero-parameter $\azero(z)$ is
jointly consistent with the sample-mean flat-rotation velocity
($\langle v_{\heat}/v_{\rm obs}\rangle=1.00\pm0.08$) and size
compactification ($\langle R_{\rm obs}/R_{0}\rangle=0.38$
observed vs.\ HEAT prediction $0.37$) of four ALMA disks at
$z\!\sim\!4.5$, and reduces to standard MOND at $z=0$ with
SPARC $\chi^{2}$ statistically indistinguishable from
constant-$\azero$ MOND ($p=0.46$; $171$ galaxies).  The
$1/(2\pi)$ proportionality constant is anchored to SPARC at
$z=0$ but cancels in the ratio $R(z)/R_{0}$, so the headline
test is invariant under the choice of normalisation.  Two new
MUSE-DARK measurements at $z\!\approx\!0.5$--$1.5$ are broadly
consistent with the predicted $\azero(z)$ shape and amplitude
\citep{Ciocan2026RAR,Ciocan2026Halo}, while the lensed bTFR
null at $z\!\approx\!1$ of \citet{Jeanneau2026} sits
$\sim 4\sigma$ below the predicted shift and is treated as the
principal extant challenge.  We present HEAT as a falsifiable
hypothesis rather than a confirmed model: pre-registered
thresholds on the exponent, the asymptote
$\mathcal{R}_{\infty}$, and the BTFR/RAR evolution specify what
upcoming surveys are required to decide the hypothesis.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
% ======================================================================

Modified Newtonian Dynamics
\citep[MOND;][]{Milgrom1983,FamaeyMcGaugh2012} describes galactic
dynamics in the low-acceleration regime with a single universal
scale $\azero\!\approx\!1.2\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$ and
predicts the radial acceleration relation
\citep[RAR;][]{McGaugh2016,Lelli2016} without dark matter.
Because $\azero$ is constant, however, MOND is silent about
cosmological evolution.  At the same time, the James Webb Space
Telescope has revealed compact, rotationally supported galaxies
at $z\!\gtrsim\!4$ \citep{Labbe2023,Carniani2024,Bunker2023}
whose structural properties motivate reopening the question of
whether $\azero$ itself carries a redshift dependence.

\citet{Milgrom1999} identified the local MOND acceleration scale
with the de~Sitter horizon temperature, $\azero=cH_0/(2\pi)$.  We
examine the testable modified-inertia hypothesis that this
identification extends covariantly to every redshift, with
$\azero$ equal to the FRW apparent-horizon surface gravity
divided by $2\pi$, $\azero(z)=c\,H(z)/(2\pi)$.  We call this
model \emph{Hubble-Emergent Acceleration Theory} (HEAT).  Its
background cosmology is held strictly degenerate with $\lcdm$
by construction, so the theory is not a competing matter sector;
all deviations from standard physics are confined to the
low-acceleration regime, and the Newtonian regime is unmodified.

While this article was being prepared, two new lines of
intermediate-redshift evidence relevant to the
$\azero(z)\propto H(z)$ hypothesis appeared.
\citet{Varasteanu2025} measure $\azero$ at $z\!<\!0.08$ from a
MIGHTEE-HI rotation-curve sample; \citet{Ciocan2026RAR}
(MUSE-DARK Paper III) infer $\azero(z)$ at $z\!\sim\!0.5$--$1.5$
from MUSE intermediate-redshift radial-acceleration relation
fits in three independent frameworks (DC14 uniform, DC14
per-galaxy, and direct MOND), reporting a $\sim 4\sigma$
positive trend in all three; the matching halo-density rise
$\rho_{s}\propto(1\!+\!z)^{0.54\pm 0.31}$ inferred from the same
sample by \citet{Ciocan2026Halo} (Paper I) is the
DM-frame counterpart of HEAT's predicted $\azero(z)$ growth.
\citet{Jeanneau2026} (MUSE-DARK Paper II), by contrast, report
no detectable evolution of the lensed bTFR zero-point at
$z\!\sim\!1$; we treat this null as the most direct extant
challenge to HEAT's BTFR prediction (\S\ref{sec:btfr}) and as a
falsification target alongside the headline size test.

This article focuses on a single falsifiable prediction chain.
In the deep-MOND equilibrium limit
$R\propto(GM_b/\azero)^{1/2}$ \citep{Milgrom1983,FamaeyMcGaugh2012},
so at fixed baryonic mass
\begin{equation}\label{eq:RofH}
  \frac{R(z)}{R_{0}}\;=\;\left[\frac{H(z)}{H_{0}}\right]^{-1/2}.
\end{equation}
We emphasise a structural property of Eq.~\ref{eq:RofH} that is
central to what follows: the ratio $R(z)/R_{0}$ depends only on
the \emph{scaling} $\azero\propto H$, not on the value of the
proportionality constant.  For any ansatz of the form
$\azero(z)=K\,H(z)$, the constant $K$ cancels identically when
the radius is normalised to its $z=0$ value, so the headline
size-evolution prediction is invariant under the choice of
normalisation.  In particular, the empirical anchor
$\azero(0)=cH_{0}/(2\pi)$ discussed in \S\ref{sec:ansatz} fixes
the absolute value of $\azero$ but does \emph{not} propagate to
Eq.~\ref{eq:RofH} or its factorised form (Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull}
below).  The $1/(2\pi)$ enters only the absolute predictions of
\S\ref{sec:alma} (flat-rotation velocities) and
\S\ref{sec:btfr} (BTFR zero-point), both of which are anchored
to SPARC at $z=0$ by construction.  Consequently the principal
test of this article -- the redshift evolution of late-type
sizes -- is genuinely free of adjustable parameters even before
any thermodynamic interpretation of $\azero$ is adopted.

Two consequences follow algebraically, with no free parameter at
any step.  The non-trivial assumptions entering Eq.~\ref{eq:RofH}
are three and explicit: (i) deep-MOND equilibrium scaling
$R\propto(GM_b/\azero)^{1/2}$, (ii) evaluation at fixed
baryonic mass across cosmic time, and (iii) a fixed local
size anchor $R_{0}$.  Throughout this article, condition~(ii) is
interpreted as a statement about \emph{mass-selected populations}
at each redshift, not about the evolutionary track of an
individual galaxy: Eq.~\ref{eq:RofH} predicts the median size of
disks of a given baryonic mass observed at redshift $z$, and the
empirical comparisons in \S\ref{sec:tests} are correspondingly
binned in stellar (or baryonic) mass at each $z$.  Departures
from any of these---evolving
mass profiles, active mergers, non-equilibrium kinematics, or
an incorrect $R_{0}$---contribute as \emph{scatter} around the
predicted curve, not as a shift in its functional form; the
$-3/4$ exponent and the $\mathcal{R}(z)$ normalisation are
therefore protected against the generic galaxy-formation
systematics that dominate individual-galaxy residuals.
\emph{First}, in the matter era
$H(z)\propto(1+z)^{3/2}$, so Eq.~\ref{eq:RofH} asymptotes to
$R(z)/R_{0}\propto(1+z)^{-3/4}$---the exponent measured by
\citet{vanderWel2014} in thousands of late-type galaxies and
confirmed at higher redshift by \citet{Allen2017}.
\emph{Second}, away from the matter asymptote the full
$[H(z)/H_{0}]^{-1/2}$ curve differs from the pure $(1+z)^{-3/4}$
power law by a normalisation factor which reduces to the closed
form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Rnorm}
  \mathcal{R}(z)\;\equiv\;
    \frac{R_{\heat}(z)/R_{0}}{(1+z)^{-3/4}}
  \;=\;
    \left[\frac{\Omega_{m}(z)}{\Omega_{m}(0)}\right]^{1/4},
\end{equation}
with $\Omega_{m}(z)\equiv\rho_{m}(z)/\rho_{\rm tot}(z)$ the
instantaneous matter density parameter.  The two consequences
combine into the single composite relation
$R_{\heat}(z)/R_{0}=(1+z)^{-3/4}\times[\Omega_{m}(z)/\Omega_{m}(0)]^{1/4}$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull} below), displaying exponent and normalisation
as separate factors.  The exponent probes the matter-era
logarithmic slope, the normalisation probes the
$\Lambda$-to-matter transition shape and asymptote; both are
fixed entirely by Planck cosmology and the $\azero\propto H(z)$
coupling.

The rest of this article formalises the derivation
(\S\ref{sec:ansatz}--\S\ref{sec:predictions}), compares the
predictions to existing data (\S\ref{sec:tests}), and
pre-registers falsification thresholds (\S\ref{sec:falsify}).
We defer additional phenomenology---a baryon-budget relief
channel for JWST $z\gtrsim 10$ candidates, collapse-time
enhancements, and the full SPARC $\chi^2$ analysis---to a longer
treatment and do not advance it as primary evidence here.

\section{Empirical scaling $\azero\propto H^{n}$ and its
  horizon-thermodynamic interpretation}\label{sec:ansatz}
% ======================================================================

We pose the question empirically rather than thermodynamically.
Within the modified-inertia family
$\azero(z)\propto H^{n}(z)$, the deep-MOND closure
$R\propto(GM_b/\azero)^{1/2}$ \citep{Milgrom1983,FamaeyMcGaugh2012}
combined with the matter-era scaling
$H(z)\propto(1+z)^{3/2}$ forces a late-type size exponent
$d\log R/d\log(1+z)=-3n/4$.  The measured value
$-0.75\!\pm\!0.05$ from the deep \citet{vanderWel2014}
late-type sample therefore pins
$n=1.00\!\pm\!0.07$ (\S\ref{sec:exp}); the family
collapses to a single empirically selected scaling.
We then ask what theoretical structure naturally selects
$n=1$, and find that horizon thermodynamics---specifically the
de~Sitter / FRW apparent-horizon temperature---provides such a
structure with no further freedom.  The remainder of this section
\emph{interprets} the empirically forced scaling rather than
deriving it independently.

\paragraph{Status of free parameters.}
Two distinct claims must be kept separate.
\emph{Redshift evolution:} zero free parameters---the
$n=1$ scaling $\azero(z)\propto H(z)$ is forced by the matter-era
size exponent (\S\ref{sec:exp}) and propagates through
Eqs.~\ref{eq:Rfull}--\ref{eq:Vfull} with no further freedom.
\emph{Absolute normalisation at $z\!=\!0$:} anchored to SPARC,
with the $1/(2\pi)$ identification matching the empirical MOND
scale to within $13\%$.  The headline observable
$R(z)/R_{0}$ depends on the first claim only (K-invariance,
\S\ref{sec:exp}); the $1/(2\pi)$ enters only the absolute value
of $\azero$ at $z\!=\!0$.

\paragraph{Horizon-thermodynamic interpretation of $n=1$.}
An observer comoving with the FRW background sees an apparent
horizon at radius $R_A(z)=c/H(z)$ with surface gravity
$\kappa_A(z)=cH(z)$ \citep{BakRey2000,CaiKim2005} and a
Hawking--Unruh temperature
$T_A=\hbar\kappa_A/(2\pi c\,k_B)=\hbar H/(2\pi k_B)$
\citep{Hawking1975,Unruh1976,GibbonsHawking1977}.  At $z=0$
\citet{Milgrom1999} observed that promoting this horizon
temperature to an acceleration,
$\kappa_A(0)/(2\pi)=cH_0/(2\pi)\!\approx\!1.04\times10^{-10}
\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$, recovers the empirical MOND scale to
within $13\%$ of the SPARC posterior
($1.2\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$;
\citealt{Lelli2016,Li2018}).  Within HEAT, horizon thermodynamics
interprets the empirically forced $n\!=\!1$ scaling as the
covariant extension of this static identification to every
redshift,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:a0}
  \azero(z)\;=\;\frac{\kappa_A(z)}{2\pi}
    \;=\;\frac{c\,H(z)}{2\pi}.
\end{equation}
The $2\pi$ is the Euclidean period that relates horizon surface
gravity to temperature in any Hawking--Unruh setting, including
the \citet{GibbonsHawking1977} de~Sitter temperature; we
emphasise, however, that the \emph{numerical} identification of
$\azero$ with $\kappa_A/(2\pi)$ rather than (e.g.) $\kappa_A$ or
$\kappa_A/\pi$ is fixed by matching the empirical SPARC value of
$\azero$ at $z=0$, not by an independent first-principles
derivation: the residual $13\%$ between the horizon-thermodynamic
value $cH_0/(2\pi)$ and the SPARC posterior \citep{Lelli2016,Li2018}
is comparable to per-galaxy scatter in $\azero$ across SPARC
sub-samples and is therefore consistent with a zero-parameter
identification at the precision currently available, but it is
\emph{anchored} rather than derived.  As a heuristic underlying
picture, an observer accelerating at rate $a$ in a de~Sitter
background sees a composite Unruh bath \citep{DeserLevin1997},
$T_{\rm tot}(a,H)=(\hbar/2\pi c\,k_B)\sqrt{a^{2}+c^{2}H^{2}}$,
whose excess above the cosmological floor $T_{dS}(H)$ becomes
quadratic in $a$ for $a\!\ll\!cH$ and maps onto deep-MOND
$v_{\rm flat}^{4}=GM_b\azero$.  We treat this mapping as an
\emph{effective} relation whose phenomenological content is
Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}; a microscopic derivation in a covariant
formulation along the lines of \citet{Hossenfelder2017} or the
thermodynamic-gravity programme of
\citet{Jacobson1995,Padmanabhan2010} is beyond the scope of
this article and does not affect the observational predictions
that follow.

The thermodynamic step is therefore \emph{interpretive}: the
ingredients (de~Sitter / FRW horizon temperature, surface gravity
$\kappa_A=cH$, Hawking--Unruh periodicity) are standard
\citep{Jacobson1995,Padmanabhan2010}, while the identification of
$\azero$ with $\kappa_A/(2\pi)$ promotes \citet{Milgrom1999}'s
static observation to a covariant statement consistent with the
empirically selected $n\!=\!1$.  A separate parameter-free
MOND-like framework---Covariant Emergent Gravity
\citep{Hossenfelder2017,HossenfelderMistele2018}---also ties
$\azero$ to cosmological quantities but predicts a substantially
weaker redshift dependence than Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}; the two
hypotheses can be distinguished observationally
(\S\ref{sec:falsify}).  HEAT's empirical contribution is to show
that Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}---with zero free parameters governing
redshift evolution---passes three independent redshift windows
without adjustment.

\section{Two matched zero-parameter predictions}\label{sec:predictions}
% ======================================================================

\subsection{Derivation of the exponent}\label{sec:exp}

In the deep-MOND regime
$R\propto(GM_b/\azero)^{1/2}$ \citep{Milgrom1983,FamaeyMcGaugh2012}.
Combined with Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}, at fixed baryonic mass this yields
Eq.~\ref{eq:RofH}.  The matter-dominated cosmological scaling
$H(z)\propto(1+z)^{3/2}$ then forces
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Rexp}
  R(z)/R_{0}\;\longrightarrow\;(1+z)^{-3/4}
  \qquad(z\gg z_{\rm eq,\Lambda}),
\end{equation}
i.e.\ the exact exponent of the late-type size--redshift
relation measured by \citet{vanderWel2014} in $3$D-HST$+$CANDELS
out to $z\!\sim\!3$ and confirmed at $z\!\sim\!3$--$5$ by
\citet{Allen2017}.  The rational exponent $-3/4$ is forced by
the three-step chain (Eq.~\ref{eq:a0} + deep-MOND kernel +
matter-era cosmology) and carries no freedom at any step.  Within
the modified-inertia family $\azero\propto H^{n}$, the matter-era
size exponent is $-3n/4$, so the \citet{vanderWel2014} measurement
of $-0.75\pm 0.05$ pins $n=1.00\pm 0.07$ and rules out the
constant-$\azero$ limit ($n=0$, standard MOND; predicted
exponent~$0$), the fractional coupling $n=1/2$ (exponent $-3/8$),
and the quadratic coupling $n=2$ (exponent $-3/2$) at
$\gtrsim 15\sigma$, $\gtrsim 7\sigma$, and $\gtrsim 15\sigma$
respectively.  Within the single-parameter family $\azero\propto
H^{n}$, $n=1$ is therefore the \emph{only} value consistent with
the measured late-type size exponent.

For completeness we note that a baseline $\lcdm$ disk-formation
calculation with
$R_{\rm disk}\propto\lambda\,R_{\rm vir}\propto H^{-2/3}$
\citep{MoMaoWhite1998} returns the matter-era exponent $-1$,
while abundance-matched fits invoking halo-concentration
evolution, adiabatic contraction, and feedback-driven angular
momentum loss return best-fit exponents in the range
$-0.6$ to $-1.0$ depending on ingredient choices
\citep{Somerville2018}.  HEAT's $-3/4$ is (a)~a rational
exponent forced by two rigid physical steps rather than an
ensemble fit, and (b)~within $1\sigma$ of the measured value
\citep{vanderWel2014}.

\subsection{Derivation of the
  normalisation}\label{sec:norm}

The matter-era power law $(1+z)^{-3/4}$ is an asymptote, not the
full HEAT prediction.  The ratio of the full
$[H(z)/H_{0}]^{-1/2}$ curve to that asymptote defines
$\mathcal{R}(z)\equiv (1+z)^{3/4}\,[H(z)/H_{0}]^{-1/2}$ and
admits a closed form.  Writing
$H(z)^{2}=H_{0}^{2}[\Omega_{m}(1+z)^{3}+\Omega_{\Lambda}]$ and
using the identity
$\Omega_{m}(z)\equiv\Omega_{m}(1+z)^{3}/E(z)^{2}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Rnorm2}
  \mathcal{R}(z)\;=\;
    \left[\frac{\Omega_{m}(z)}{\Omega_{m}(0)}\right]^{1/4}.
\end{equation}
$\mathcal{R}(z)$ is bounded, monotonic, and fully parameter-free:
$\mathcal{R}(0)=1$ by the local anchor, and
$\mathcal{R}(z)\to\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.335$ in the
matter era.  The transition shape between the two limits is the
$\Lambda$-to-matter interpolation around
$z_{\rm eq,\Lambda}\!\approx\!0.3$, with no galaxy-formation
freedom anywhere in the curve.  Numerically, with Planck-fixed
$\Omega_m=0.315$,
\begin{equation*}
  \mathcal{R}(z)\;=\;
    \{1.00,\,1.18,\,1.25,\,1.29,\,1.31,\,1.32,\,1.33\}
\end{equation*}
at $z=\{0,\,0.5,\,0.9,\,1.5,\,2.0,\,3.0,\,4.0\}$.

Collecting the two derivations, Eqs.~\ref{eq:RofH} and
\ref{eq:Rnorm2} together imply a single composite prediction
for the late-type size--redshift relation,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Rfull}
  \boxed{\;\;\frac{R_{\heat}(z)}{R_{0}}
   \;=\;(1+z)^{-3/4}\;\times\;
   \left[\frac{\Omega_{m}(z)}{\Omega_{m}(0)}\right]^{1/4}\;\;}
\end{equation}
in which the first factor is the matter-era asymptote
(\S\ref{sec:exp}) and the second is the closed-form
$\Lambda$-to-matter normalisation correction (\S\ref{sec:norm}),
asymptoting to $\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.335$.  Equation
\ref{eq:Rfull} is algebraically equivalent to Eq.~\ref{eq:RofH}
but displays the two empirically accessible faces of the
prediction---exponent and normalisation---as separate factors,
each forced by the chain
Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}\,$+$\,deep-MOND kernel\,$+$\,Planck cosmology
with no fitted quantity.  It is Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull} that upcoming
Euclid, Roman, and JWST mass-complete size catalogues will test.

Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2} is the \emph{core novel prediction of this
article}.  Importantly it is not a fit: the $z=0$ anchor is definitional
(both curves equal $R_{0}$ at $z=0$ by construction), and the
matter-era asymptote $\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}$ is fixed by Planck.
Only the \emph{shape} of $\mathcal{R}(z)$ at $z>0$ carries
non-trivial content, but that shape is also fully determined.
Any coupling $\azero\propto H^{n}$ with $n\neq 1$, or a matter-only
coupling $\azero\propto H_{m}(z)$, predicts a different
asymptote; the observed saturation near $1.34$ at $z\gtrsim 2$
\citep[Table~\ref{tab:interz} below and][]{vanderWel2014} is
consistent with $n=1$ and disfavours $n\neq 1$ at the
qualitative level of current data.

\paragraph{Distinguishing feature relative to galaxy-formation
physics.}
The strongest claim of this article is the following: no
$\Omega_{\Lambda}$-independent mechanism reproduces both the
asymptote $\mathcal{R}_{\infty}\!\approx\!1.34$ and the location
of the $\Lambda$-sourced break at
$z_{\rm eq,\Lambda}\!\approx\!0.3$---these are simultaneous
signatures of the cosmological constant entering the size
relation through $\Omega_{m}(z)$, not through any galaxy-scale
physics.  The closed-form prediction Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2} is the
principal discriminant of this article because it does not arise
from a single background quantity in standard $\lcdm$
disk-formation modelling without invoking coordinated redshift
evolution across several baryonic ingredients.  The exponent $-3/4$ alone does not
establish this: abundance-matched $\lcdm$ disk-formation models
with tuned feedback and angular-momentum loss can reach
matter-era exponents anywhere in the range $-0.6$ to $-1.0$
\citep{Somerville2018,MoMaoWhite1998}, and the measured $-0.75$
lies inside that band.  The \emph{normalisation curve} is
different in kind.  Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2} is a function of
$\Omega_{m}(z)$ alone, with asymptote
$\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.34$ and a specific
$\Lambda$-sourced transition shape centred on
$z_{\rm eq,\Lambda}\!\approx\!0.3$.  Baryonic galaxy-formation
physics---feedback efficiencies, merger rates, spin-parameter
evolution, adiabatic contraction---is not directly tied to
$\Omega_{\Lambda}$: these processes act on halo time-scales
$\propto H(z)^{-1}$ that already absorb the expansion history,
but standard implementations do not single out the
$[\Omega_{m}(z)/\Omega_{m}(0)]^{1/4}$ shape as a generic outcome.
Reproducing Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2} within $\lcdm$ is therefore
\emph{not a generic outcome of disk-formation modelling}: it
would arise only if multiple, mutually independent baryonic
processes happened to combine into a $z$-profile coincident
with $\mathcal{R}(z)$, rather than emerging from a single
background quantity.  HEAT, by contrast, delivers the full
$\mathcal{R}(z)$ with no adjustable parameter as an algebraic
consequence of Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}.  Measuring $\mathcal{R}(z)$ to
percent-level accuracy over $0\!\lesssim\!z\!\lesssim\!3$
therefore probes a structure that is non-generic in $\lcdm$
galaxy-formation models without invoking a specific,
$\Omega_\Lambda$-dependent combination of baryonic ingredients.

\paragraph{Robustness to named astrophysical systematics.}
We list the three baryonic effects most likely to be invoked
against Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2} and indicate why each fails to
reproduce its specific shape.  (a)~\emph{Evolving gas fractions
and stellar/baryonic mass selection}: gas-rich high-$z$ disks
have larger $f_{\rm gas}$ and therefore systematically lower
$M_\star/M_b$, but the corresponding bias in size at fixed
$M_\star$ is smooth, monotone, and tracks the halo time-scale
$\propto H(z)^{-1}$; it does not single out the
$\Lambda$-sourced break near $z_{\rm eq,\Lambda}\!\approx\!0.3$
that is the distinctive feature of $\mathcal{R}(z)$.
(b)~\emph{Merger-induced size growth}: dry mergers inflate sizes
but predominantly broaden the dispersion at fixed mass rather
than coherently shift the binned mean by the
$\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.34$ factor that
Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2} forces, and recent merger-rate measurements
\citep{Conselice2014,Duncan2019} show comparable rates across
$z\!\sim\!1$--$3$ rather than the
$\Lambda$-modulated profile required.
(c)~\emph{Local size-anchor calibration drift}: the
\citet{Shen2003} versus \citet{vanderWel2014} local anchors
differ by $\sim 20\%$, comparable to the $\sim 25$--$33\%$
elevation but with no redshift dependence, so it cannot mimic
the rise from $\mathcal{R}(0)=1$ to
$\mathcal{R}_{\infty}\!\approx\!1.34$.  Reproducing the joint
shape (location of break, asymptote, monotonicity) within
$\lcdm$ thus requires the three effects to conspire in a
coordinated, $\Omega_\Lambda$-dependent fashion, rather than
each contributing as an independent broadening.

\paragraph{Quantitative mass-selection mimic.}  We make
systematic~(a) explicit.  Suppose a static-MOND universe (constant
$\azero$) in which the underlying baryonic mass at fixed
\emph{observed} stellar mass evolves as a power law,
$M_{b}(z)=M_{b}(0)\,(1+z)^{\alpha}$, capturing evolving gas
fractions and stellar/baryonic mass selection in a one-parameter
caricature.  The deep-MOND equilibrium
$R\propto(GM_{b}/\azero)^{1/2}$ then gives, at fixed observed
$M_{\star}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mimic}
  \mathcal{R}_{\rm mimic}(z;\alpha)\;\equiv\;
   \frac{R(z)/R_{0}}{(1+z)^{-3/4}}
   \;=\;(1+z)^{\alpha/2}.
\end{equation}
Eq.~\ref{eq:mimic} is monotone, unbounded, and
$\Lambda$-independent.  HEAT's $\mathcal{R}(z)$ is bounded,
saturates at $\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.335$, and has a
$\Lambda$-sourced break at
$z_{\rm eq,\Lambda}\!\approx\!0.3$.  No single $\alpha$
reproduces both shape features.  The value
$\alpha\!\approx\!0.36$ tuned to match the asymptote at
$z\!=\!4$ \emph{by construction} still under-predicts the rise
at $z\!=\!0.5$ by $\sim 10\%$ (mimic $1.08$ vs HEAT $1.18$) and
overshoots the saturation at $z\!\gtrsim\!4$ as $z\to\infty$.
Figure~\ref{fig:mass_sel} visualises the family
$\alpha\!\in\![0.0,0.5]$ together with the asymptote-tuned
mimic and the HEAT closed form: the mimics share the
$z\!=\!0$ anchor with $\mathcal{R}(z)$ but diverge from it
both at the break ($z\!\sim\!0.3$, where they rise too slowly)
and at high redshift ($z\!\gtrsim\!4$, where they fail to
saturate).  This rules out a one-parameter mass-evolution
explanation of $\mathcal{R}(z)$ at the percent level and
quantifies systematic~(a): an $\Omega_\Lambda$-independent
power-law $M_{b}(z)$ cannot reproduce the
$\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}$ asymptote without simultaneously
violating the binned-mean rise at the break.

\subsection{Scaling family}\label{sec:family}

The same factorisation applied to any deep-MOND observable
$\mathcal{O}\propto\azero^{p}$ yields the redshift dependence
$\mathcal{O}(z)/\mathcal{O}(0)=[H(z)/H_{0}]^{p}$ with matter-era
asymptote $(1+z)^{3p/2}$ and normalisation
$[\Omega_{m}(z)/\Omega_{m}(0)]^{-p/2}$.  Each exponent below is
rational and algebraically forced.  The size and velocity rows
carry exponents of \emph{opposite} sign, which is what makes the
joint velocity--size cross-check of \S\ref{sec:alma}
structurally informative even at small $N$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{One scaling, four observable
consequences.}
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item[$\bullet$] \textit{$R_{\rm eff}$ at fixed $M_b$:}
  $p=-1/2$, so $R\propto[H(z)/H_0]^{-1/2}$ and
  $R\propto(1+z)^{-3/4}$ in the matter era.  This is the
  primary size-evolution shape test.
\item[$\bullet$] \textit{$v_{\rm flat}$ at fixed $M_b$:}
  $p=+1/4$, so $v\propto[H(z)/H_0]^{1/4}$ and
  $v\propto(1+z)^{3/8}$ in the matter era.  This is the
  velocity consistency check.
\item[$\bullet$] \textit{BTFR zero-point:} $p=-1$, so the
  locally anchored BTFR zero-point evolves as
  $[H(z)/H_0]^{-1}$.  This tests the same coupling with an
  independent observable.
\item[$\bullet$] \textit{RAR knee:} $p=+1$, so
  $g_{\rm knee}=\azero\propto H(z)$.  This is the direct
  absolute-acceleration test.
\end{itemize}

These four channels are not equivalent under a global rescaling
$\azero\!\to\!K\,c\,H(z)$.  Replacing $1/(2\pi)$ by a constant
$K$ rescales every absolute MOND acceleration, but it cancels
from the redshift ratios $R(z)/R_{0}$, $v(z)/v_{0}$, and the
locally anchored BTFR zero-point evolution.  The size, velocity,
and BTFR-evolution rows therefore probe the \emph{shape}
$\azero(z)/\azero(0)=H(z)/H_{0}$, while the direct RAR
measurement of \S\ref{sec:a0z} is the only entry that
constrains the absolute normalisation.  The size row is tested
against \citet{vanderWel2014}, \citet{Allen2017}, and the
calibrated \citet{Allen2025} shape check; the BTFR row carries
the \citet{Ubler2017} support and the \citet{Jeanneau2026}
challenge; the RAR row is tested directly by
\citet{Ciocan2026RAR}.

Applying the factorisation of Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull} to the
flat-rotation velocity ($p=+1/4$) gives the composite
zero-parameter prediction
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Vfull}
  \boxed{\;\;\frac{v_{\rm flat}(z)}{v_{0}}
   \;=\;(1+z)^{+3/8}\;\times\;
   \left[\frac{\Omega_{m}(z)}{\Omega_{m}(0)}\right]^{-1/8}\;\;}
\end{equation}
with $z\!=\!0$ anchor $v_{0}\!=\!(GM_{b}\azero(0))^{1/4}$, a
velocity normalisation
$\mathcal{V}(z)\!\equiv\![\Omega_{m}(z)/\Omega_{m}(0)]^{-1/8}$,
and matter-era asymptote
$\mathcal{V}_{\infty}\!=\!\Omega_{m}^{+1/8}\!\approx\!0.866$.
The opposite signs of the exponents in $\mathcal{R}(z)$ ($+1/4$)
and $\mathcal{V}(z)$ ($-1/8$) make their ratio
$\mathcal{R}(z)/\mathcal{V}(z)=[\Omega_{m}(z)/\Omega_{m}(0)]^{3/8}$
a further parameter-free cosmological probe, asymptoting to
$\Omega_{m}^{-3/8}\!\approx\!1.54$.

We stress, however, that the observational leverage of
Eq.~\ref{eq:Vfull} is substantially weaker than that of
Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull} on current data: the predicted normalisation
shift is only $\mathcal{V}_{\infty}\!-\!1\!\approx\!-13\%$ (versus
$\mathcal{R}_{\infty}\!-\!1\!\approx\!+34\%$ for sizes), below
the $15$--$20\%$ per-galaxy systematic floor on high-$z$ rotation
velocities from inclination, beam-smearing, and
turbulent-broadening subtraction (\S\ref{sec:alma}).  The
velocity channel therefore functions here as a \emph{consistency
requirement}---the ALMA sample mean of \S\ref{sec:alma} is
compatible with Eq.~\ref{eq:Vfull}---rather than as a precision
test of $\mathcal{V}(z)$.  A definitive test of the velocity
normalisation requires mass-binned, kinematically clean samples
with $\gtrsim 50$ disks per redshift bin, which upcoming ALMA
large programmes and JWST/NIRSpec IFU campaigns are expected to
deliver.

% ======================================================================
\section{Observational tests}\label{sec:tests}
% ======================================================================

We organise the empirical comparisons that follow into an
explicit prediction hierarchy.  The \emph{primary} test is the
shape and asymptote of the late-type size--redshift relation
$R(z)/R_{0}$ (\S\ref{sec:interz}), which is supported by the
largest sample ($\sim 10^{4}$ galaxies; \citealt{vanderWel2014})
and tests the headline prediction Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull} directly.
The \emph{secondary} test is the redshift evolution of the BTFR
zero-point (\S\ref{sec:btfr}, using \citealt{Ubler2017}), which
probes the same coupling $\azero\propto H$ through an
independent observable.  The ALMA $z\!\sim\!4.5$ joint
velocity--size sample (\S\ref{sec:alma}, $N\!=\!4$) is included
as a \emph{supporting} consistency check rather than a
statistical test, because its sample size precludes a precision
constraint on Eq.~\ref{eq:Vfull}.  Pre-registered falsification
thresholds---on the exponent, the asymptote
$\mathcal{R}_{\infty}$, and the BTFR/RAR evolution---are
collected separately in \S\ref{sec:falsify}.

\subsection{Late-type size evolution at
  $z\approx 0.9$--$8$}\label{sec:interz}

We compare the composite prediction Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull} to the
empirical late-type size--redshift fit of \citet{vanderWel2014},
extended into the ZFOURGE regime by \citet{Allen2017}, anchored
at $R_{0}\!\approx\!4\,\mathrm{kpc}$ at $\log M_{\star}=10$
\citep{Shen2003}.  The first factor of Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull} is
tested by the exponent; the second factor is tested by the
binned-mean normalisation offset.
Table~\ref{tab:interz} lists HEAT's $R(z)/R_{0}$ and the
corresponding empirical $(1+z)^{-0.75}$ values over
$z=0.9$--$3.5$.  The two are consistent on the exponent to
within $1\%$ across the range, while the HEAT curve lies
systematically $25$--$33\%$ above the matter-era empirical
power law---consistent within the local-anchor systematic
band with the behaviour predicted by Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2},
which forces
$\mathcal{R}(z\geq 3)\to\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.34$.
The binned-mean ratio column is consistent with the
$\mathcal{R}(z)$ profile of \S\ref{sec:norm} at the
$\sim 20$--$30\%$ precision set by local size-calibration
systematics (the \citealt{Shen2003} vs.\ \citealt{vanderWel2014}
local anchors alone differ by $\sim 20\%$).

A complementary high-$z$ check is provided by the JWST/NIRCam
rest-optical size--mass relation of \citet{Allen2025}.  At fixed
$M_{\star}=5\times10^{10}\,M_{\odot}$ they report
$\log R_{e}=(-0.807\pm 0.026)\log(1+z)+(0.947\pm 0.014)$ over
$0\!<\!z\!<\!9$.  When evaluated at the same local late-type
anchor used here,
$R_{0}=4(M_{\star}/10^{10}\,M_{\odot})^{0.22}=5.70$\,kpc, the
relation tracks $[H(z)/H_{0}]^{-1/2}$ to within
$2.7$--$8.4\%$ over $z=3$--$8$, and the HEAT-implied mean local
anchor inferred from the Allen high-$z$ points is $6.00$\,kpc,
only $5.3\%$ above the adopted one.  This is a calibrated shape
check, not an independent absolute-normalisation confirmation:
the \citet{Allen2025} extrapolated $z\!=\!0$ intercept is
$10^{0.947}\!=\!8.85$\,kpc, about $55\%$ larger than the local
anchor used in this article.  The numerical comparison at
$z\!\in\!\{3,4.5,6,8\}$ is reproduced by the
\texttt{\_allen2025\_size\_calibration} block of
\texttt{publication/jwst\_early\_galaxies.py}.

We emphasise that this is a consistency check, not a precision
test of the functional form: we do not quote a significance in
$\sigma$ units because a proper likelihood on the joint
size--mass--redshift surface is not available in the literature,
and the current data neither confirm nor exclude
$\mathcal{R}(z)$ against the matter-only baseline at the level
required.  The apparent plateau of the binned-mean ratio near
$1.3$ across $z\!\in\![2,3.5]$ sits within the local-anchor
systematic band; resolving it to the percent level is the
specific task that upcoming surveys are expected to accomplish.  Figure~\ref{fig:norm} visualises both
features: panel~(a) shows the HEAT and matter-era curves sharing
a common $z\!=\!0$ anchor and diverging by $\mathcal{R}(z)$,
while panel~(b) isolates $\mathcal{R}(z)$ and its asymptote.

\subsection{ALMA joint velocity--size consistency check at
  $z\sim 4.5$ ($N=4$, internal cross-check)}\label{sec:alma}

All four galaxies at $z>4$ with published resolved ALMA
rotation velocities---SPT0418-47 at $z=4.22$ \citep{Rizzo2020}
and three disks at $z\sim 4.5$
\citep{RomanOliveira2023,RomanOliveira2024}---admit an
\emph{internal consistency check} (not a statistical test, given
$N=4$) of Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}.  Because
$v_{\rm flat}\propto\azero^{+1/4}$ and $R\propto\azero^{-1/2}$
scale with opposite powers of the acceleration, a joint match
on both axes probes the functional form of $\azero(z)$, not
merely its normalisation: a rescaling that improves one
observable generically worsens the other.  We treat the
resulting numbers as supporting consistency rather than as
independent evidence; the headline test remains the
$\sim 10^{4}$-galaxy size shape of \S\ref{sec:interz}.

Using published total baryonic masses and the globally fixed
$\azero(z)$ we compute the deep-MOND flat velocity
$v_{\rm flat}=(GM_b\,\azero)^{1/4}$ and the equilibrium
compactification $R/R_{0}=[\azero(z)/\azero(0)]^{-1/2}$ for
each galaxy (Table~\ref{tab:alma}).  The zero-parameter
sample-mean ratios are
$\langle v_{\heat}/v_{\rm obs}\rangle=1.00$ and
$\langle R_{\rm obs}/R_{0}\rangle_{\rm pred}=0.37$, jointly
consistent with the observed sample means ($1.00$ and $0.38$
respectively) on both axes within the $N{=}4$ sample-mean
envelope.  The corresponding constant-$\azero$ MOND
velocity mean is $0.63$.  Leave-one-out resampling gives
$1.00\pm 0.08$ (HEAT) versus $0.63\pm 0.05$ (MOND) on the
velocity axis; the HEAT advantage survives removal of any
single galaxy.  Per-galaxy $v_{\rm obs}$ values carry
intrinsic systematic uncertainties at the $15$--$20\%$ level
from inclination, beam smearing, and turbulent-broadening
subtraction, so the residual scatter in individual HEAT-to-
observed ratios ($0.75$--$1.36$) is consistent with a
zero-parameter prediction combined with a realistic
observational error budget.  Formal statistical significance
is precluded by $N=4$; the definitive test requires
$\gtrsim 10$ resolved rotation curves at $z>3$, which
upcoming ALMA large programmes will provide.

\subsection{BTFR zero-point: consistency with the predicted
  trend}\label{sec:btfr}

Combining the deep-MOND baryonic Tully--Fisher relation
$V_{\rm flat}^{4}=GM_{b}\,\azero$
\citep{Milgrom1983,FamaeyMcGaugh2012} with the
HEAT ansatz Eq.~\ref{eq:a0} implies a zero-parameter shift of
the BTFR zero-point at fixed rotation velocity,
\begin{equation}
  \Delta\log_{10}M_{b}(z)=-\log_{10}\!\left[\frac{H(z)}{H_{0}}\right],
  \label{eq:btfr-evo}
\end{equation}
while constant-$\azero$ MOND predicts
$\Delta\log_{10}M_{b}(z)=0$ independently of redshift.  As with
the size ratio $\mathcal{R}(z)$ of \S\ref{sec:exp}, we use the
presently available heterogeneous sample only to check whether
the direction and amplitude of the predicted trend are
consistent with the data; a decisive quantitative test awaits a
homogeneous cosmic-noon rotation-curve sample.  Projecting the
four $z>4$ ALMA kinematic sources of Table~\ref{tab:alma} onto
the axis Eq.~\ref{eq:btfr-evo} (Figure~\ref{fig:btfr}) yields
$\Delta_{\rm obs}$ values of $-0.87$ dex (CRISTAL-22, $z=4.53$),
$-0.33$ dex (DC-881725, $z=4.56$), and $-1.03$ and $-1.36$ dex
for the caveated SPT0418-47 and SGP38326 systems, against a
HEAT prediction of $\simeq-0.86$ dex at $z\!\sim\!4.5$.  The SPARC
$z=0$ anchor (171 galaxies, \citealt{Lelli2016}) sits on the
HEAT curve within the \citet{Lelli2019} intrinsic scatter of
$\sim 0.10$ dex, by construction of the horizon-thermodynamic
anchor; CRISTAL-22 --- the most rotation-dominated
equilibrium system in the high-$z$ sample --- sits on the
zero-parameter HEAT curve to within the per-galaxy
systematics.  The remaining points scatter above and below
the curve at amplitudes consistent with the known
merger/lensed/clumpy-disk systematics, and the overall trend
is in the direction predicted by Eq.~\ref{eq:btfr-evo} and
opposite to the constant-$\azero$ MOND null.  A homogeneous
cosmic-noon ($z\sim 1$--$2.5$) sample of $\sim 10$
rotation-dominated disks with individual $\lesssim 15\%$
velocity precision would convert this trend consistency into a
quantitative discrimination between HEAT, CEG, and
constant-$\azero$ MOND at $>3\sigma$ at fixed baryonic mass.

\paragraph{Tension with the lensed bTFR null at $z\!\sim\!1$.}
\citet{Jeanneau2026} (MUSE-DARK Paper II) report no detectable
evolution of the lensed bTFR zero-point at $z\!\sim\!1$,
$\Delta\log_{10}M_{b}\!=\!0.00\!\pm\!0.05$\,dex, against the
HEAT prediction $-\log_{10}[H(z\!=\!1)/H_{0}]\!\approx\!-0.26$\,dex.
Taken at face value this is a $\sim 4\sigma$ challenge to
Eq.~\ref{eq:btfr-evo} and we list it as such in
\S\ref{sec:falsify}.  Two caveats temper the immediate
interpretation but do not dissolve the tension.  First, the
Jeanneau sample is strongly lensed, so individual baryonic-mass
estimates carry $\gtrsim 0.2$\,dex systematic uncertainty from
source-plane reconstruction and the assumed disk mass-to-light
ratio (the same $\sim 0.2$\,dex shift that
\citealt{Ciocan2026Halo} (Paper I) explore explicitly in their
Appendix~C systematic test); a coherent $+0.2$ to $+0.45$\,dex
zero-point bias relative to the SPARC anchor is not excluded.
Second, the Jeanneau zero-point is derived assuming a
\emph{static} BTFR slope and $z=0$ scatter, which absorbs part
of any redshift dependence into the inferred normalisation.  The
direct $\azero(z)$ measurement of \citet{Ciocan2026RAR} (Paper
III), discussed in \S\ref{sec:a0z}, is consistent with the HEAT
\emph{shape} (free-$K$ $\chi^{2}\!=\!7.2$ for $5$ dof) and with
HEAT's zero-point in the MOND framework (Paper III App.~E) to
within $1\%$, so the trilogy as a whole supports the
$\azero\propto H(z)$ coupling on at least two of three
independent observables; the Jeanneau bTFR null remains the
single most demanding falsifier of HEAT in the current
literature.

\subsection{Direct $\azero(z)$ test at intermediate
  redshift}\label{sec:a0z}

The redshift evolution of $\azero$ is the single feature on
which HEAT differs from constant-$\azero$ MOND.  The MUSE-DARK
collaboration has now provided a direct measurement of $\azero$
at $z\!\sim\!0.5$--$1.5$ from intermediate-redshift radial
acceleration relation (RAR) fits to a sample of MUSE
spectro-imaging targets in the Hubble Ultra-Deep Field
\citep{Ciocan2026RAR}.  We compare the resulting $\azero(z)$
points against three reference scalings: (i) the zero-parameter
HEAT prediction $\azero(z)\!=\!cH(z)/(2\pi)$; (ii) the constant
$\azero(0)\!\equiv\!1.20\!\times\!10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$
of \citet{McGaugh2016}; and (iii) the linear-in-$z$
parameterisations $\azero(z)\!=\!a_{0}(0)\!+\!a_{1}\,z$ that
Paper~III adopts as a phenomenological summary in three
independent fitting frameworks (DC14 uniform, DC14 per-galaxy,
and direct MOND).

\paragraph{Shape of $\azero(z)$.}  Across the full set of six
data points (SPARC at $z\!=\!0$, \citealt{Varasteanu2025} at
$z\!\sim\!0.04$, and the four \citealt{Ciocan2026RAR} bins at
$z\!\sim\!0.55$, $0.78$, $1.05$, $1.30$), the constant-$\azero$
MOND null is excluded at $\chi^{2}\!\simeq\!212$ for $6$ data
points; HEAT's zero-parameter prediction with
$K\!=\!1/(2\pi)$ gives $\chi^{2}\!\simeq\!79$, dominated by a
joint $\sim 13\%$ z=0 anchor offset and a $\sim 25\%$ slope
under-prediction at $z\!>\!0.5$.  Crucially, allowing only the
proportionality constant $K$ in $\azero(z)\!=\!K\,c\,H(z)$ to
float and re-fitting yields $\chi^{2}\!=\!7.2$ for $5$ degrees
of freedom with a best-fit $K\!=\!0.215\!\approx\!1.35\,/\,(2\pi)$.
That is, the \emph{shape} of the data is consistent with
HEAT's $H(z)$ scaling at the $\sim 1\sigma$ level, and the only
residual tension lies in the absolute normalisation, which is
the very quantity the K-invariance argument of \S\ref{sec:exp}
shows does not enter the headline observable $R(z)/R_{0}$.
By comparison, the three Ciocan linear-in-$z$ fits give
$\chi^{2}\!=\!28$--$34$, all substantially worse fits to the
same six points than the free-$K$ HEAT shape despite carrying
\emph{one extra} fitted parameter; the full goodness-of-fit
ladder is collected in Table~\ref{tab:a0z_chi2}.  The
constant-$\azero$ null remains, vastly worse than every
$\azero \propto H^{n}$-flavoured alternative tested.

The Hubble-scaling agreement is most cleanly seen as a
shape-convergence statement: anchored at the SPARC $z\!=\!0$
value, the ratio
$[\azero^{\rm obs}(z)/\azero^{\rm SPARC}]/[H(z)/H_{0}]$ falls
monotonically across the four \citet{Ciocan2026RAR} bins from
$1.22$ at $z\!=\!0.55$ to $1.06$ at $z\!=\!1.30$, indicating
that the data approaches the HEAT-predicted Hubble scaling at
high redshift to within $\sim 6\%$.

\paragraph{Methodology-split $\chi^{2}$.}  The two
local-redshift anchors enter the residual through different
inversions of different observables: SPARC and the
\citet{Ciocan2026RAR} bins are derived from rotation-curve
modelling under a MOND-flavoured interpolation function, while
\citet{Varasteanu2025} infers $\azero$ from the slope and
intercept of an HI baryonic Tully--Fisher relation.
\citet{Rodrigues2018} and \citet{McGaugh2018} document
methodology-dependent shifts of order $\sim 30\%$ between
these two inversions on overlapping samples; consistent with
that, \citet{Varasteanu2025} sits $3.5\sigma$ above the SPARC
RC-derived value at essentially the same redshift, and is the
single largest contributor to $\chi^{2}$ for every
$\azero\!\propto\!H^{n}$-flavoured model we consider.  We
therefore report the goodness-of-fit ladder twice: on the full
$N\!=\!6$ sample and on the rotation-curve-derived
$N\!=\!5$ subset (Table~\ref{tab:a0z_chi2}, second
$\chi^{2}$ column).  On the RC-only cohort the
$K\!=\!1/(2\pi)$ HEAT prediction gives
$\chi^{2}\!=\!55.6$, the free-$K$ HEAT shape achieves
$\chi^{2}\!=\!2.9$ for $4$ degrees of freedom (with
$K\!\approx\!1.32\,K_{\rm HEAT}$, statistically
indistinguishable from the full-sample free-$K$ fit), and the
constant-$\azero$ MOND null remains excluded at
$\chi^{2}\!=\!197$.  The relative ordering of the models is
preserved on both cohorts.

\paragraph{Cross-framework normalisation match.}  In the MOND
framework adopted in Paper~III Appendix~E, the linear-fit
intercept is $\azero(z\!=\!0)\!=\!1.03\!\pm\!0.05\!\times\!10^{-10}\,
\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$.  HEAT's zero-parameter prediction at
$z\!=\!0$ is $cH_{0}/(2\pi)\!=\!1.04\!\times\!10^{-10}\,
\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$.  These two numbers agree to within $1\%$,
and this is the apples-to-apples comparison: both invert the
same MOND interpolation function on the same observed
kinematics, so any methodology-dependent normalisation drift
is differenced out.  The DC14 uniform fit
($1.00\!\pm\!0.04$) and the DC14 per-galaxy fit
($1.05\!\pm\!0.05$) bracket the HEAT value within their
$1\sigma$ envelope, again with the MOND interpolation
function used inside the disk--halo decomposition.

The SPARC RAR-fit value
$\azero^{\rm SPARC}(0)\!=\!1.20\!\pm\!0.26$ is a different
inversion of partially overlapping physics: a galaxy-by-galaxy
RAR median rather than a population-level interpolation-function
fit.  \citet{Rodrigues2018} and \citet{McGaugh2018} document
methodology-dependent shifts of order $\sim 30\%$ between the
two reductions on the same SPARC subsample, of the same
direction and roughly the same magnitude as the
$\sim 13\%$ offset between HEAT's $1.04$ and SPARC's $1.20$.
HEAT's parameter-free prediction therefore agrees with the
framework-self-consistent (MOND-interpolation) z=0 anchor at
$1\%$ and remains within $1\sigma$ of the SPARC RAR-fit
anchor; we treat the two anchors as bounding the
methodology-dependent normalisation rather than as a single
common datum.

\paragraph{Appendix robustness.}  The positive $\azero(z)$
trend in Paper~III is not tied to a single halo
parameterisation.  Paper~III's appendix repeats the inversion
under a uniform DC14 prescription, per-galaxy DC14 fits, and a
MOND-framework fit; all three recover an increasing acceleration
scale across the MUSE-DARK redshift interval, with a common
qualitative shape but methodology-dependent intercepts.  This
is why Fig.~\ref{fig:a0z} carries those reductions as external
calibration curves rather than as independent data points, and
why Table~\ref{tab:a0z_chi2} reports their $\chi^{2}$ alongside
the strict zero-parameter HEAT prediction.

\paragraph{Stellar-mass systematic.}  A natural objection is that
the apparent $\azero(z)$ rise reflects an unmodelled mass
systematic in the high-$z$ Ciocan sample: low signal-to-noise
disk decomposition at $z\!\sim\!1$ might overestimate
$M_{\star}$, biasing the inferred $\azero$ upward.  Paper~III
Appendix~C explicitly tests this by applying coherent
$+0.2$ and $+0.45$\,dex shifts to the disk mass-to-light ratio
of the entire sample; the recovered $\azero(z)$ trend persists
in both cases, ruling out this systematic as the source of the
positive slope.  The Paper~III analysis therefore stands
independent of the M/L assumption to the precision currently
quoted.

\paragraph{Halo-density counterpart from Paper~I.}  An
independent corroboration comes from the same MUSE-DARK sample
analysed in the DM frame.  \citet{Ciocan2026Halo} (Paper~I) fit
six DM density profiles to the full sample of resolved
rotation curves at $z\!\sim\!0.85$ and report
$\rho_{s}\!\propto\!(1\!+\!z)^{0.54\pm 0.31}$ for the NFW-equivalent
characteristic density, i.e.\ a $\Delta\log_{10}\rho_{s}
\!\approx\!0.30\!\pm\!0.10$\,dex elevation at $z\!=\!0.85$
relative to local SPARC-equivalent halos.  In any
modified-inertia framework the surface-density scale of inferred
DM halos is set by the prevailing $\azero(z)$, so a positive
$\Delta\log_{10}\rho_{s}(z)$ at fixed baryonic content is the
DM-frame counterpart of HEAT's predicted $\azero(z)$ rise.
HEAT predicts
$\Delta\log_{10}\rho_{s}(z)\!\sim\!\log_{10}[H(z)/H_{0}]
\!=\!0.21$\,dex at $z\!=\!0.85$, within $0.9\sigma$ of the
Paper~I measurement.  Figure~\ref{fig:a0z} summarises the
$\azero(z)$ comparison and the
$\Delta\log_{10}\Sigma_{\rm DM}(z)$ counterpart.

\paragraph{Status.}  The intermediate-redshift RAR data of
\citet{Ciocan2026RAR} are inconsistent with the
constant-$\azero$ MOND null at $\gtrsim 4\sigma$, consistent
with the HEAT \emph{shape} once a single normalisation $K$ is
allowed to float, and---in the MOND-framework reduction---
extrapolate to a $z\!=\!0$ intercept agreeing with the
zero-parameter HEAT value to within $1\%$.  Combined with the
Paper~I halo-density elevation and the Paper~II bTFR null
discussed in \S\ref{sec:btfr}, the trilogy provides two of
three independent observational signatures of an evolving
$\azero(z)$, with the lensed bTFR null remaining the principal
challenge listed in the falsification programme of
\S\ref{sec:falsify}.

\subsection{Local anchor: consistency with SPARC at
  $z\approx 0$}\label{sec:sparc}

The ansatz Eq.~\ref{eq:a0} reduces to standard MOND at $z=0$ by
construction; the role of the $z=0$ comparison is therefore to
certify that the $\azero(z)$ extension has not damaged existing
deep-MOND phenomenology.  Against 171 SPARC galaxies
\citep{Lelli2016} with fixed mass-to-light ratios
($\Upsilon_{\rm disk}=0.5$, $\Upsilon_{\rm bulge}=0.7\,\Msun/L_\odot$)
and no per-galaxy tuning, HEAT and constant-$\azero$ MOND are
statistically indistinguishable (paired Wilcoxon $p=0.46$);
HEAT achieves a lower $\chi^{2}_{\rm red}$ than MOND in
$98/171=57\%$ of galaxies and the same holds separately for
the Newtonian ($g_{\rm bar}>\azero$) and deep-MOND
($g_{\rm bar}<\azero$) sub-samples
(Figure~\ref{fig:sparc}).  The horizon-thermodynamic value
$\azero(0)=cH_{0}/(2\pi)=1.04\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$
is adopted from Eq.~\ref{eq:a0} rather than fitted and lies
within the $\sim 20\%$ per-galaxy scatter of the SPARC
posterior \citep{Lelli2016,Li2018}.

\section{Falsification}\label{sec:falsify}
% ======================================================================

HEAT makes concrete, pre-registered predictions that distinguish
it from both $\lcdm$ and constant-$\azero$ MOND.  The general
strategy of testing MOND variants via the redshift dependence
of the radial acceleration relation was first advocated by
\citet{HossenfelderMistele2018} as the cleanest discriminator
among MOND-family frameworks; the thresholds below are HEAT's
specific, zero-parameter instantiation within that strategy.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Evolving BTFR (headline future test).}  HEAT
    predicts $v_{\rm flat}(z)/v_{\rm flat}(0)=[\azero(z)/\azero(0)]^{1/4}$,
    with $v_{\rm flat}(z=4)/v_{\rm flat}(0)\approx1.59$.
    Constant-$\azero$ MOND predicts $1.00$ at all epochs; CEG
    \citep{Hossenfelder2017,HossenfelderMistele2018} predicts
    a substantially weaker evolution.  A sample of
    $\gtrsim 10$ BTFR points at $z=3$--$6$ with individual
    $15\%$ velocity precision discriminates all three at
    $>3\sigma$.  HEAT is falsified if the BTFR normalisation
    at $z\!\sim\!4$ is measured below $1.30$.
  \item \textbf{Size-exponent test.}  HEAT predicts
    $R(z)/R_{0}\propto(1+z)^{-3/4}$ in the matter era
    (\S\ref{sec:exp}).  A measured late-type exponent
    significantly steeper than $-1$ or shallower than
    $-0.5$ between $z=0.5$ and $z=3$ would rule HEAT out.
  \item \textbf{Normalisation asymptote and transition shape.}
    Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2} forces
    $\mathcal{R}_{\infty}\equiv\lim_{z\to\infty}\mathcal{R}(z)=
    \Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.34$ and the specific
    $\lcdm$-fixed transition shape between
    $\mathcal{R}(0)=1$ and $\mathcal{R}_{\infty}$.  An
    asymptote measured outside $[1.2,\,1.5]$, or a transition
    shape inconsistent with the
    $[\Omega_{m}(z)/\Omega_{m}(0)]^{1/4}$ profile at the
    percent level in Euclid/Roman data, would disfavour the
    $\azero\propto H(z)$ coupling independently of test 2.
  \item \textbf{Redshift-dependent RAR.}  At fixed
    $g_{\rm bar}$, $g_{\rm obs}\propto\azero^{1/2}$, displacing
    the RAR intercept by a factor $\sim 2.5$ at $z=4$ from
    the zero-parameter ansatz.  A measured displacement below
    $1.3$ at $z\gtrsim 3$ rules HEAT out while remaining
    consistent with constant-$\azero$ MOND.
  \item \textbf{Direct $\azero(z\!=\!2)$ measurement.}  HEAT
    predicts $\azero(z\!=\!2)\!=\!3.03\,\azero(0)\!\approx\!3.15\!
    \times\!10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$.  An RAR-based
    determination of $\azero$ from a homogeneous
    $z\!\sim\!2$ rotation-curve sample (e.g.\ extending the
    \citealt{Ciocan2026RAR} pipeline to JWST/NIRSpec IFU disks)
    that places $\azero(z\!=\!2)\!<\!2.0\!\times\!10^{-10}\,
    \mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$ at $\geq 3\sigma$ falsifies the
    $\azero\propto H(z)$ ansatz independently of the size and
    BTFR channels.
  \item \textbf{Direct $\azero(z\!=\!3)$ measurement.}  Likewise,
    HEAT predicts $\azero(z\!=\!3)\!\approx\!4.75\!\times\!
    10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$.  A measurement
    $<\!2.5\!\times\!10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$ at the same
    significance falsifies the ansatz at the high-redshift
    matter-era asymptote, complementing test~5.
  \item \textbf{Direct HI-based bTFR threshold at $z\!\sim\!1$.}
    HEAT predicts a bTFR zero-point shift
    $\Delta\log_{10}M_{b}\!=\!-\log_{10}[H(z\!=\!1)/H_{0}]\!\approx\!
    -0.255$\,dex at $z\!=\!1$.  A homogeneous direct HI-based
    bTFR sample (i.e.\ velocities measured from rotation
    rather than absorption-line stellar dynamics, removing the
    Jeanneau+2026 source-plane systematic) of
    $\gtrsim 30$ disks at $z\!\sim\!1$ that returns
    $|\Delta\log_{10}M_{b}|\!<\!0.05$\,dex centred on $0.00$ at
    $\geq 3\sigma$ confidence falsifies Eq.~\ref{eq:btfr-evo}.
    The current Jeanneau+2026 lensed-bTFR null
    ($0.00\!\pm\!0.05$\,dex) is consistent with this falsifying
    threshold and we treat it as a provisional challenge that
    awaits independent direct HI-based confirmation.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusions}
% ======================================================================

Identifying the MOND acceleration scale with the FRW
apparent-horizon surface gravity divided by $2\pi$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}) yields, at fixed baryonic mass, two
zero-parameter redshift-evolution predictions for late-type
galaxy structure that follow directly from the ansatz without
further fitting.  They combine into the headline factorised
relation
$R_{\heat}(z)/R_{0}=(1+z)^{-3/4}\times[\Omega_{m}(z)/\Omega_{m}(0)]^{1/4}$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:Rfull}), in which exponent and normalisation enter
as separately testable factors and in which the $1/(2\pi)$
anchor cancels exactly --- so the headline test is
parameter-free under the weaker assumption
$\azero(z)\propto H(z)$ alone.  The matter-era exponent
$R\propto(1+z)^{-3/4}$ is the galactic-scale signature of
de~Sitter horizon thermodynamics and is consistent at $1\sigma$
with the \citet{vanderWel2014} measurement in thousands of
galaxies.  The closed-form normalisation
$\mathcal{R}(z)=[\Omega_{m}(z)/\Omega_{m}(0)]^{1/4}$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2}) is a bounded, monotonic function of
redshift with a specific shape and a Planck-fixed matter-era
asymptote $\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}\!\approx\!1.34$; current data are
consistent with it at the $\sim 20$--$30\%$ level set by local
size-calibration systematics but do not yet discriminate it
from the matter-only baseline.  Upcoming Euclid/Roman data
will push the per-bin uncertainty to the percent level,
converting the present consistency check into a quantitative
test of the $\azero\propto H(z)$ coupling within the $\lcdm$
expansion history on structure scales.  An internal consistency
check is provided by the joint velocity--size cross-check on
four ALMA disks at $z\!\sim\!4.5$ (Table~\ref{tab:alma};
$N\!=\!4$, not a statistical test) and by statistical
equivalence with constant-$\azero$ MOND on SPARC at $z=0$
(\S\ref{sec:sparc}).

A covariant completion of Eq.~\ref{eq:a0} analogous to that of
TeVeS \citep{Bekenstein2004}, Covariant Emergent Gravity
\citep{Hossenfelder2017}, or the relativistic MOND family of
\citet{Skordis2021} is not attempted here and remains the
principal theoretical open problem.  We therefore do not
claim HEAT is confirmed; rather, we offer a zero-parameter,
empirically anchored alternative to constant-$\azero$ MOND
whose seven pre-registered falsifiers (\S\ref{sec:falsify})
can each be resolved within the next survey cycle.

The empirical status of this article is therefore conditional
and mixed.  Current data on the headline size relation
$R(z)/R_{0}$ (\S\ref{sec:interz}) establish consistency at the
$\sim 20$--$30\%$ level set by local size-calibration systematics
but do not yet discriminate $\mathcal{R}(z)$ from the
matter-only baseline.  The recent MUSE-DARK trilogy adds two
independent intermediate-redshift signals broadly supportive of
the $\azero\propto H(z)$ coupling
\citep{Ciocan2026RAR,Ciocan2026Halo} together with one
challenging null \citep{Jeanneau2026}: the Paper~III RAR-based
direct $\azero(z)$ measurement is consistent with the HEAT
shape (\S\ref{sec:a0z}, free-$K$ $\chi^{2}\!=\!7.2$ for $5$ dof)
and matches the HEAT zero-parameter normalisation in the MOND
framework to within $1\%$; the Paper~I halo-density rise
$\rho_{s}\!\propto\!(1\!+\!z)^{0.54\pm 0.31}$ is consistent
within $0.9\sigma$ with HEAT's predicted $\Delta\log_{10}\Sigma_{\rm
DM}(z)$; the Paper~II lensed bTFR null at $z\!\sim\!1$, by
contrast, sits $\sim 4\sigma$ below the HEAT-predicted shift
and is the principal extant challenge to Eq.~\ref{eq:btfr-evo},
with caveats discussed in \S\ref{sec:btfr}.  The hypothesis
stands or falls on the joint percent-level measurement of
$R(z)/R_{0}$ over $0\!\lesssim\!z\!\lesssim\!3$ from
homogeneous, mass-complete Euclid and Roman samples and on the
direct HI-based bTFR test at $z\!\sim\!1$: a confirmed rise
from $\mathcal{R}(0)\!=\!1$ to
$\mathcal{R}_{\infty}\!\approx\!1.34$ with the specific
$\Omega_{\Lambda}$-sourced shape predicted by
Eq.~\ref{eq:Rnorm2} together with a confirmed
$\Delta\log_{10}M_{b}(z\!=\!1)\!\approx\!-0.26$\,dex would
provide strong evidence for the
$\azero\propto H(z)$ coupling on structure scales, while a
measured exponent outside $-0.75\pm 0.05$ at fixed baryonic
mass, an asymptote $\mathcal{R}_{\infty}$ inconsistent with
$\Omega_{m}^{-1/4}$ at the percent level, or a confirmed
direct HI-based bTFR null at $z\!\sim\!1$ would falsify
Eq.~\ref{eq:a0}.  The full falsification programme---including
the new $\azero(z\!=\!2)$, $\azero(z\!=\!3)$, and direct HI bTFR
thresholds---is collected in \S\ref{sec:falsify}.
